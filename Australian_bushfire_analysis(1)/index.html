Australian bushfire analysis

The Australian bushfire has led to a massive loss to wildlife, forest area and has even caused human casualties, including firefighters from the U.S. It has even affected the air quality in nearby cities such as Sydney and Melbourne. We will take a look at fire data obtained from NASA satellite's MODIS and VIIRS.

This is the first section

The first chart uses a map to indicate the brightness of a bushfire in each location. The brightness is the flame pixel measured in Kelvin. The higher the brightness, the greater the fire in the area.

This is the second section

The second chart uses a histogram to show the distribution of the number of bushfires that occurred every day between September 1, 2019 and December 31, 2019.

This is the third section

The third chart uses a histogram to show the distribution of the number of bushfire satellite types that occurred every day between September 1, 2019 and December 31, 2019.

This is the fourth section

The fourth chart uses a discount graph to show the distribution of the number of bushfires that occurred every day in each month between September 2019 and December 2019.

This is the fifth section

The fifth chart uses an area graph to show the average distribution of scans for bushfires that occurred every day between September 1, 2019 and December 31, 2019.
