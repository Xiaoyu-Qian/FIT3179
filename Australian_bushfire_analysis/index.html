Australian bushfire analysis

The Australian bushfire has led to a massive loss to wildlife, forest area and has even caused human casualties, including firefighters from the U.S. It has even affected the air quality in nearby cities such as Sydney and Melbourne. We will take a look at fire data obtained from NASA satellite's MODIS and VIIRS.

The first section

The first part uses a map to illustrate the intensity of the bushfires at each location. Brightness is the flame pixel measured in Kelvin, the higher it indicates the greater the fire in the area.

The second section

The second part uses a histogram to show the distribution of the number of bushfires that occurred every day from September 1, 2019 to December 31, 2019.

The third section

The third part uses a histogram to compare the distribution of the proportion of satellite types that have recorded bushfires in two different Satellite types (Terra and Aqua) during the period from September 1, 2019 to December 31, 2019.

The fourth section

The fourth part uses a discount chart to show the difference in the number of bushfires each month between September 2019 and December 2019.

The fifth section

The fifth part uses the heatmap to indicate the average brightness between 1st and 30th during the period from September 1, 2019 to December 31, 2019.
